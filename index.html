About

I am a PhD researcher at RMIT University, Melbourne, investigating clustering methods for skewed, low-separation datasets using novel Bayesian algorithms. My work spans theoretical development, computational implementation in R/JAGS, and real-world applications including wildfire hazard mapping.

With a Chancellor's Gold Medal in MPhil Statistics and an MSc from the University of Glasgow, I bring over five years of university-level teaching experience across Pakistan, the United Kingdom, and Australia.

Research

PhD thesis: Clustering skewed datasets using newly proposed Bayesian algorithm

01

Bayesian Clustering for Skewed Data

Developed a new Bayesian clustering algorithm for skewed, low-separation data using JAGS in R. Benchmarked on 30 real-world datasets, improving clustering performance by up to 62–70% on agreement and quality metrics.

02

Wildfire Hazard Mapping (NSW)

Produced operational wildfire hazard maps for New South Wales (2019–2025). Improved true alarm rates by 4% and reduced false alarms by 9% compared to the next-best method.

03

Fuzzy Bayesian Framework

Extended the Bayesian model to a fuzzy framework for uncertainty analysis across 30 datasets. Highest clustering performance on more than 70% of datasets evaluated.
